# Engineering Governance

Engineering governance is the control layer that makes engineering work observable, testable, and trusted.

It is not status theater. It is the rules, telemetry, review paths, and ownership model that stop delivery risk from becoming hidden rework.

Canonical TeamStation research:

https://teamstation.dev/research/articles

## Key Questions

- Which engineering controls should exist before AI workflows scale?
- How do CTOs know if acceptance rules are strong enough?
- What governance signals reduce repair cost?
- How does distributed engineering stay trusted across handoffs?
